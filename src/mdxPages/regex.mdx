## **What is RegEx?**  
Regular Expressions (**RegEx**) are patterns used to match **text, numbers, symbols, or specific patterns** in a string.  

**Example:**  
- Find all numbers: `/\d+/`
- Extract email addresses: `/\w+@\w+\.\w+/`
- Replace spaces with dashes: `.replace(/\s+/g, "-")`

## **Step 2: Quantifiers (How Many Times a Pattern Appears)**
| Symbol | Meaning | Example |
|--------|---------|---------|
| `+` | **One or more** occurrences | `"abc123"` â†’ `/\d+/` matches `"123"` |
| `*` | **Zero or more** occurrences | `"aaa"` â†’ `/a*/` matches `"aaa"` |
| `?` | **Zero or one** occurrence (optional) | `"color"` â†’ `/colou?r/` matches `"color"` & `"colour"` |
| `{n}` | **Exactly `n` times** | `"1234"` â†’ `/\d{3}/` matches `"123"` |
| `{n,}` | **At least `n` times** | `"1234"` â†’ `/\d{2,}/` matches `"1234"` |
| `{n,m}` | **Between `n` and `m` times** | `"1234"` â†’ `/\d{2,3}/` matches `"123"` |
